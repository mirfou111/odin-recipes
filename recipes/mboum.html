YASSA recipe


Brief Description:

Poulet Yassa is a Senegalese dish made of chicken that has been marinated in a mixture of garlic, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, and spices.

It has become very popular throughout West Africa and in other parts of the world.


Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 7 onions
  • 3 lemons
  • 1 tablespoon of mustard (optional)
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 fresh chili pepper
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper

Process

  1. Wash the chicken. In Senegal, we usually wash it with lemon and salt to remove the strong odor.
  2. Using a food processor, blend the garlic cloves, black pepper, half of the chopped bell pepper, and the chili peppers. You'll get a paste that will be used as seasoning.
  3. Cut the chicken into medium-sized pieces and make incisions in each piece.
  4. Marinate the chicken with half of the seasoning paste, a generous tablespoon of mustard, 2 lemons, black pepper, a little salt, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  5. Grill the chicken in the oven or, ideally, on a barbecue.
  6. Cut the onions into cubes or slices, as you prefer. In a pot, heat some oil. Once the oil is slightly hot, add the chopped onions. Stir to prevent them from sticking to the bottom of the pot. Add a bay leaf and the chili pepper.
  7. Keep an eye on the onions as they cook: when they turn translucent, add the rest of the seasoning paste, salt (not too much), and black pepper. Add the juice of a large lemon and a little water.
  8. Stir well, then let it simmer for about 30 minutes on low heat.
  9. Add the grilled chicken pieces into the sauce.
  10. Serve with white rice.
